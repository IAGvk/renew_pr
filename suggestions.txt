## A) Funded spaces with clear demand (build wedges or partner-first GTM)

### 1) **VPP Enrollment & Dispatch Orchestrator for C\&I**

**What**: An agent that onboards commercial sites (batteries, thermostats, EV chargers), qualifies them against grid program rules, auto-handles M\&V data, and executes DR/VPP dispatch playbooks.  
**Why now**: VPPs are scaling fast (US DOE pegs existing VPP capacity at 30–60 GW and calls them “at an inflection point”), and aggregators like **Leap** keep striking big partnerships (e.g., with Enel NA). Programs are expanding regionally; automation to enroll/operate assets is valuable.   
**Agentic angle**: Multi‑agent “site onboarding” (data intake & device mapping), “program suitability” (tariff/program matching), and “dispatch executor” (translating DR events to device-level setpoints via OpenADR/DER APIs).   
**v0 in 4–6 weeks**: [\[energy.gov\]](https://www.energy.gov/edf/virtual-power-plants-projects), [\[enelnorthamerica.com\]](https://www.enelnorthamerica.com/about-us/newsroom/search-press/press/2025/12/leap-vpps), [\[energy-storage.news\]](https://www.energy-storage.news/us-virtual-power-plant-programmes-advance-with-new-partnerships-and-investment/) [\[openadr.org\]](https://www.openadr.org/)

*   **Data & APIs**: Program metadata (utility PDFs→parsers), OpenADR test VENs; devices via vendor SDKs. [\[openadr.org\]](https://www.openadr.org/)
*   **Stack**: FastAPI + Postgres (site/program registry), an **ops agent** with tools for meter data QA, baseline calc, and OpenADR event handling; simple React admin.
*   **Pilot ICP**: Mid‑size cold storage/retail portfolios (repeatable loads + good DR performance).

***

### 2) **“Autobidder‑Lite” for <10 MW Assets (NEM/CAISO/ ERCOT)**

**What**: An intelligent bidding agent for small storage/solar‑storage owners who can’t justify enterprise platforms. It learns constraints (warranties, cycles), forecasts prices, and submits bids/orders autonomously.  
**Why now**: Digital bidding platforms (Fluence **Mosaic**, Fluence IQ) and Tesla **Autobidder** prove revenue uplift, and vendors are moving down‑market (<50 MW). The long tail is under‑served.   
**Agentic angle**: One agent handles price/imbalance forecasts; another enforces asset constraints; a trader agent composes bids with risk tolerance and market rules.  
**v0 in 4–6 weeks**: [\[fluenceenergy.com\]](https://fluenceenergy.com/fluence-iq-digital-platform/), [\[blog.kowatek.com\]](https://blog.kowatek.com/2025/05/15/fluence-eyes-emerging-market-for-battery-projects-under-50-mw/), [\[tasautomation.com\]](https://www.tasautomation.com/mosaic-bidding/)

*   **Data**: Public ISO price feeds + backfill; asset telemetry emulator; warranty constraints input.
*   **Stack**: Forecasts (XGBoost/LSTM), rules engine, and a market adaptor for sandbox submission (start with paper trading).

### 3) **24/7 CFE Procurement Copilot (Granular Certificates + Storage Sizing)**

**What**: Agent that builds a 24/7 carbon‑free procurement plan: identifies hourly gaps, suggests GCs/PPAs/storage, and executes trades in **granular certificate** markets.  
**Why now**: 24/7 CFE is going mainstream—LevelTen launched a GC trading initiative with Google/Microsoft; EnergyTag has accredited issuers (**FlexiDAO, Energinet, Granular Energy**) and Google is scaling hourly matching.   
**Agentic angle**: “Gap‑finder” agent matching hourly demand to supply, “sourcing” agent that picks optimal GCs/PPAs, and “verification” agent that assembles auditable evidence.   
**v0 in 4–6 weeks**: [\[datacenter...namics.com\]](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/google-and-microsoft-back-247-carbon-free-energy-marketplace/), [\[utilitydive.com\]](https://www.utilitydive.com/news/google-microsoft-constellation-levelten-hourly-REC-marketplace/702637/), [\[energytag.org\]](https://energytag.org/energytag-accredits-first-granular-certificate-issuers-marking-a-major-milestone-for-hourly-clean-energy-tracking/), [\[datacenter...namics.com\]](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/google-to-expand-247-clean-energy-matching-with-flexidao/), [\[granular-energy.com\]](https://www.granular-energy.com/insights/granular-energy-becomes-accrediated-issuer-of-granular-certificates-under-energytag) [\[energytag.org\]](https://energytag.org/projects/matching-for-googles-24-7-goal/)

*   **Data**: Hourly demand, emissions factors (Electricity Maps), GC issuer APIs (FlexiDAO/Granular Energy docs). [\[energytag.org\]](https://energytag.org/projects/matching-for-googles-24-7-goal/), [\[energytag.org\]](https://energytag.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/PUBLIC-Flexidao-GC-Scheme-Protocol_Configuration-3.pdf)
*   **Stack**: Portfolio simulator (Python), contract catalog (Postgres), LangGraph agents to propose/execute hourly fills; export an audit bundle (PDF).

### 4) **EV Fleet Managed‑Charging Agent (Utility‑ & OEM‑aware)**

**Why now**: **WeaveGrid** raised $28M (Toyota/Woven Capital), pointing to surging utility/OEM integrations; **Octopus Intelligent** tariffs show consumer appetite and ecosystem tooling; OpenADR/“Grid Aware Charging” guidelines clarify signaling.   
**Agentic angle**: Dispatcher agent optimizes charge windows vs. price/carbon/DSO capacity frames; SLA agent ensures minimum SOC by shift start.  
**v0 in 4–6 weeks**: [\[weavegrid.com\]](https://www.weavegrid.com/news/weavegrid-raises-strategic-investment-led-by-woven-capital), [\[prnewswire.com\]](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/weavegrid-raises-28m-including-strategic-investment-led-by-woven-capital-302326942.html), [\[octopus.energy\]](https://octopus.energy/blog/intelligent-octopus-go-charge-limit/), [\[elaad.nl\]](https://elaad.nl/wp-content/uploads/downloads/OpenADR-Residential_Flex_OpenADR-Feb-2026.pdf)

## B) Under‑served but ripe (data shows pain; solutions are fragmented)

### 5) **Interconnection & Siting Copilot (US/EU now; AU module next)**

**What**: Agent that triages interconnection options, models queue risks, and proposes siting/size/hybridization to minimize **time‑to‑COD** and **curtailment risk**.  
**Why now**: LBNL shows **\~10,300 projects** with **1,400 GW gen + 890 GW storage** stuck and **median queue times >4 years**; Australia’s AEMO warns of rising curtailment and publishes **Enhanced Locational Information** to steer developers.   
**Agentic angle**: “Grid‑risk” agent that ingests queue/constraint data; “design” agent that toggles hybrid/storage & POIs to meet IRR and curtailment thresholds.  
**v0 in 4–6 weeks**: [\[emp.lbl.gov\]](https://emp.lbl.gov/queues), [\[osti.gov\]](https://www.osti.gov/biblio/3008763), [\[pv-magazin...tralia.com\]](https://www.pv-magazine-australia.com/2025/07/14/aemo-forecasts-increased-solar-curtailment-in-nem/), [\[aemo.com.au\]](https://aemo.com.au/-/media/files/electricity/nem/planning_and_forecasting/enhanced-locational-information/2024-eli-report.pdf)

*   **Data**: LBNL queues, ISO maps; for AU: AEMO ELI PDFs + constraint/hosting metrics, press on 2024 curtailment patterns. [\[emp.lbl.gov\]](https://emp.lbl.gov/queues), [\[aemo.com.au\]](https://aemo.com.au/-/media/files/electricity/nem/planning_and_forecasting/enhanced-locational-information/2024-eli-report.pdf), [\[pv-tech.org\]](https://www.pv-tech.org/australian-solar-pv-power-plants-see-curtailment-above-25-in-2024/)
*   **Stack**: Spatial DB (PostGIS), curtailment heuristics, and a deal‑memo generator for investment committees.

### 6) **Renewables O\&M Copilot (PV + Wind) with Agentic Workflows**

**What**: An O\&M agent that converts alarms + SCADA into prioritized work orders, suggests spares, and sequences crews—backed by predictive models (e.g., inverter derate, gearbox health).  
**Why now**: NREL’s PV O\&M and wind O\&M research emphasize data standardization, cost modeling, and predictive programs to cut downtime; growing digitalization (CMB/drones) creates the dataset the agent needs.   
**Agentic angle**: “Triage” agent ranks faults by revenue risk; “planning” agent creates route‑optimized schedules and spares lists; “learning” agent updates failure priors.  
**v0 in 4–6 weeks**: [\[docs.nrel.gov\]](https://docs.nrel.gov/docs/fy24osti/85819.pdf), [\[docs.nrel.gov\]](https://docs.nrel.gov/docs/fy23osti/83712.pdf), [\[docs.nrel.gov\]](https://docs.nrel.gov/docs/fy23osti/84457.pdf)

*   **Data**: Sample SCADA streams; BOM libraries; historical tickets.
*   **Stack**: Feature store for alarms, prophet/XGBoost baselines, and LangGraph planner to draft work orders with confidence scores.

### 7) **Grid‑Side Capacity Uplift Advisor (Dynamic Line Rating + Grid Enhancing Tech)**

**What**: Agent that recommends where to deploy DLR sensors and estimates MW unlock, wildfire risk reduction, and NPV vs reconductoring.  
**Why now**: **LineVision** (DLR vendor) has raised capital and published large deployments with **AES**; DOE GRIP is funding GETs; AU’s ARENA funded DLR pilots with **Infravision**.   
**Agentic angle**: “Uplift modeler” computes ambient‑adjusted ampacity, then a “budget agent” sequences deployments to hit congestion constraints.  
**v0 in 4–6 weeks**: [\[trysignalbase.com\]](https://www.trysignalbase.com/news/funding/linevision-secures-45-million-in-funding-to-power-the-future-of-grid-flexibility-and-resilience), [\[aes.com\]](https://www.aes.com/newsroom/aes-and-linevision-release-results-study-assessing-largest-single-deployment-dynamic-line), [\[utilitydive.com\]](https://www.utilitydive.com/news/doe-grid-resilience-funding-grip/730255/), [\[arena.gov.au\]](https://arena.gov.au/projects/infravision-next-generation-line-monitoring-system-demonstration/)

*   **Data**: Weather/terrain layers, line specs (public filings), congestion/curtailment hotspots.
*   **Stack**: Python notebooks for ampacity scenarios; a simple planner that outputs a ranked deployment roadmap and ROI.

### 8) **Hydrogen Electrolyzer Flex Agent (Soak Curtailment; Earn Ancillaries)**

**What**: Agent that schedules PEM electrolyzers against prices/curtailment and co‑optimizes product commitments with grid services.  
**Why now**: IEA’s **Global Hydrogen Review 2024** catalogs electrolyzer growth; DOE’s 2024 program record shows modeled **$5–$7/kg** LCOH (no subsidies) under certain capex/CF assumptions; ESIG’s 2024 analysis details how flexible H₂ provides grid value.   
**Agentic angle**: “Market” agent (price & carbon), “plant” agent (ramp limits, stack degradation), and “contract” agent to ensure offtake SLAs.  
**v0 in 4–6 weeks**: [\[iea.org\]](https://www.iea.org/reports/global-hydrogen-review-2024), [\[hydrogen.energy.gov\]](https://www.hydrogen.energy.gov/docs/hydrogenprogramlibraries/pdfs/24005-clean-hydrogen-production-cost-pem-electrolyzer.pdf?sfvrsn=8cb10889_1), [\[esig.energy\]](https://www.esig.energy/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/ESIG-Flexibility-Hydrogen-Power-System-Models-report-2024.pdf)

*   **Data**: Day‑ahead/intraday prices; curtailment signals; simple electrolyzer/reservoir model.
*   **Stack**: Co‑optimization (Pyomo), with guardrails for stack life and H₂ delivery windows.

## C) “Now-easy” overlooked builds (ship quickly; iterate with real users)

### 9) **Home/SMB Prosumer Agent (Home Assistant + OpenADR)**

**What**: A plug‑in agent that orchestrates PV, battery, hot‑water and EV in Home Assistant; it subscribes to DR events via OpenADR and automates bidding/response.  
**Why now**: Community components and articles show HA’s energy orchestration is mature; OpenADR is standardizing residential flexibility and publishes program tooling.   
**Agentic angle**: “Tariff” agent picks an hourly schedule; “event” agent reacts to DSO signals; “explain” agent writes user‑friendly recaps (savings, carbon).  
**v0 in 4–6 weeks**: [\[github.com\]](https://github.com/Voder/ha-energy-manager), [\[thehomesmarthome.com\]](https://thehomesmarthome.com/home-assistant-advanced-grid-integration-energy-management/), [\[openadr.org\]](https://www.openadr.org/)

*   **Stack**: HA custom integration (Python), rule graphs, and a simple onboarding UI.
*   **Pilot ICP**: Solar‑plus‑battery homeowners on dynamic tariffs; later, small hospitality sites.
