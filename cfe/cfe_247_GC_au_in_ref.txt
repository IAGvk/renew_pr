# 🇦🇺 **AUSTRALIA — How 24/7 CFE Works When Small Consumers Get STCs (Not EACs/RECs/GOs/I‑RECs)**

### ❗ What STCs actually are

*   **STCs = upfront subsidy**, not a usage‑tracking certificate.
*   They are **created once**, based on the *expected* lifetime generation of a small‑scale PV system (up to 100 kW).
*   They **do not represent an hourly, monthly or annual unit of electricity**.
*   They **cannot be used for hourly matching**, 24/7 CFE, or claims of renewable usage.

So rooftop solar households or small farmers in Australia **do not have any certificate stream** usable for 24/7 matching.

### ❗ What is available for large projects

*   **Large‑scale Generation Certificates (LGCs)** *do* exist for plants >100 kW under the LRET scheme.
*   LGCs are annual MWh‑based certificates, still **not granular** (not hourly).
*   A large corporate buyer may source LGCs via PPAs or GreenPower‑type products.

### ✔ So how does your 24/7 CFE Copilot work in Australia?

### **Case 1 — HOUSEHOLDS, FARMS, SMEs (STC recipients)**

Because STCs are not hourly or even annual certificates, your app **cannot** use STCs for CFE matching.

**What your product does instead:**

1.  Pull **hourly consumption** from smart meters (Australia has near‑universal interval metering).
2.  Pull **hourly rooftop PV generation** (via inverter APIs).
3.  Calculate **self‑consumption** vs **grid imports** for each hour.
4.  Provide a **24/7 CFE score based on the user’s own generation**, NOT certificates.
5.  Identify **dirty hours** → recommend:
    *   add battery
    *   shift load
    *   subscribe to a retailer offering 24/7 matched energy (emerging market)
    *   consider community solar participation
    *   consider corporate-backed GC products (in future)

👉 **Bottom line:**  
For household/small business Australia, **your app becomes a pure hourly matching tool** using measured solar generation—not a certificate ingestion tool.

***

### **Case 2 — CORPORATE AUSTRALIA (LGCs + PPAs)**

Corporate PPAs generate **LGCs** they can retire annually.

Your CFE Copilot can:

1.  Ingest **hourly PPA generation**.
2.  Ingest LGCs (annual certificates).
3.  Convert annual LGCs into **hourly Granular Certificates** *via a GC issuer such as FlexiDAO or Granular Energy.*
    *   This works because GC issuers already support **Configuration #3** conversion of monthly/annual EACs into hourly GCs by validating production data.
    *   (Based on how GC issuers work globally; FlexiDAO converts EACs + hourly production data into GCs.) [\[flexidao.com\]](https://www.flexidao.com/resources/flexidao-energytag)
4.  Perform the same 24/7 matching workflow as in the US/EU.

👉 **Bottom line:**  
Corporate Australia *can* participate in 24/7 CFE by funneling their LGC‑based PPAs through a GC issuer.

# 🇮🇳 **INDIA — Where Net Metering Dominates for Retail & Only Corporate/I-REC Projects Generate Certificates**

### **Retail Consumers (Homes, Apartments, SMEs)**

*   India provides **net metering / gross metering**, *not renewable certificates* for small solar sites.
*   Retail consumers **cannot produce RECs, GOs or I‑RECs** unless they register as a designated generator (which they usually don’t).

### ✔ So how does your CFE Copilot work for Indian households/SMEs?

Same as Australia’s STC segment:

**Your app uses:**

*   smart meter hourly consumption
*   inverter hourly generation
*   net‑metering import/export logs

Then it computes:

*   hourly self‑consumption
*   hourly renewable share
*   hourly grid carbon intensity (using imported emission factors if available)

**No certificates involved.**

***

### **Corporate India — The Only Segment With Certificates (I‑RECs)**

Corporate India may:

*   build on‑site solar/wind farms
*   invest in captive renewable generation
*   purchase **I‑RECs** from domestic registered renewable plants
*   use **open access PPAs**

Only these entities receive **EACs (I‑RECs)** that can be converted into GCs.

Your CFE Copilot workflow:

1.  **Ingest I‑RECs** from the registry.
2.  Collect **hourly production** from the corporate’s own RE plant or PPA operator.
3.  Pass both into a GC issuer like FlexiDAO/Granular Energy (Configuration #3 style conversion).
4.  Once GCs exist, full hourly matching → dirty‑hour gap → GC procurement suggestions.

👉 **Bottom line:**  
Corporate India can do full 24/7 CFE through the same EAC→GC conversion process used globally (same as Google’s global initiative with FlexiDAO: converting energy purchases across 7 countries into hourly GCs). [\[flexidao.com\]](https://www.flexidao.com/resources/google-portfolio-gc-conversion)
