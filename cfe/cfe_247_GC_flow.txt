# ✅ **1. Who owns the solar/wind/hydro asset that gets certificates?**

**Short answer:**  
Owners can be **any renewable generator** whose production is eligible for Energy Attribute Certificates (EACs) and/or Granular Certificates (GCs):

*   A **residential homeowner with rooftop PV**
*   A **small farmer** with a 50 kW–1 MW solar array
*   A **commercial/industrial facility** with rooftop PV
*   A **utility‑scale solar/wind developer**
*   A **PPA project operator** (often a developer or asset owner)

**EnergyTag and GC issuers do NOT restrict by size.**  
GCs can be issued to **any generator who already has renewable energy production data + corresponding EACs.**

📌 **Evidence:**

*   GC issuers such as **Energinet** issue GCs directly “to producers in Denmark” under Configuration 1. [\[energytag.org\]](https://energytag.org/energytag-accredits-first-granular-certificate-issuers-marking-a-major-milestone-for-hourly-clean-energy-tracking/)
*   **FlexiDAO** issues GCs globally by converting existing EACs + hourly production data regardless of generator type. [\[energytag.org\]](https://energytag.org/energytag-accredits-first-granular-certificate-issuers-marking-a-major-milestone-for-hourly-clean-energy-tracking/)

So yes — your assumption is correct.

# ✅ **2. How does the generator get Granular Certificates (GCs)?**

There are **two main ways**, depending on the type of issuer.

***

## **Path A — Direct Hourly GC Issuance by the Grid Operator / Official Issuer**

Used in countries like Denmark.

*   The generator is already registered in the national EAC registry.
*   The TSO (Energinet) directly issues hourly GCs for each MWh produced.
*   The TSO has direct access to metered production data → no extra work for the generator.

📌 **Evidence:** Energinet issues GCs directly as a “Configuration 1” issuer under EnergyTag. [\[energytag.org\]](https://energytag.org/energytag-accredits-first-granular-certificate-issuers-marking-a-major-milestone-for-hourly-clean-energy-tracking/)

***

## **Path B — GC Issuance by a Third‑Party Platform (FlexiDAO / Granular Energy)**

Used globally (47+ countries for FlexiDAO, global for Granular Energy).

Steps:

1.  **Generator provides:**
    *   Monthly/annual **EACs** (RECs, GOs, I‑RECs, etc.)
    *   Hourly production meter data (from inverter logs, SCADA, or utility metering)

2.  **GC Issuer validates the pair:**
    *   Each EAC (monthly/annual) is “broken down” into hourly certificates as long as hourly metered production confirms generation.
    *   This is part of EnergyTag’s Configuration #3 method.

3.  **Issuer creates & registers GCs:**
    *   Each GC is timestamped to the exact hour of generation.
    *   Each GC inherits attributes from the underlying EAC.

📌 **Evidence:**  
FlexiDAO “turns traditional monthly energy certificates (EACs) into fully accredited, time‑stamped GCs… using hourly production data” under Configuration #3.   
Granular Energy also issues verified hourly GCs via Configuration #3 globally. [\[flexidao.com\]](https://www.flexidao.com/resources/flexidao-energytag) [\[granular-energy.com\]](https://www.granular-energy.com/insights/granular-energy-becomes-accrediated-issuer-of-granular-certificates-under-energytag)

# ✅ **3. How do GC Issuers validate and verify certificates before issuing?**

GC issuers perform strict validation as part of the EnergyTag standard.

## **Validation steps (documented)**

### **1. Verify underlying EACs**

*   The EAC must be valid, unexpired, and assigned to a specific beneficiary.
*   GC issuers ensure no double counting — a principle enforced by EnergyTag.

### **2. Verify hourly production data**

FlexiDAO example:

*   Meter data is checked against production sources.
*   Hourly production is matched to the volume of the EAC.
*   Ensures the sum of hourly GCs never exceeds MWh represented by the EAC.
*   Creates a “unique, verifiable link” between the meter data and the EAC. [\[flexidao.com\]](https://www.flexidao.com/resources/google-portfolio-gc-conversion)

### **3. Independent auditor certification**

EnergyTag accreditation requires:

*   Systems assessed by **independent auditors (Enunda)**.
*   Validators examine the issuance, registry, matching, and double‑counting safeguards. [\[energytag.org\]](https://energytag.org/energytag-accredits-first-granular-certificate-issuers-marking-a-major-milestone-for-hourly-clean-energy-tracking/)

### **4. Registry & governance rules**

EnergyTag’s standard governs:

*   Issuance
*   Cancellation
*   Tracking
*   Expiry
*   Tamper‑proof audit trails

(These are documented in EnergyTag’s scheme protocol sections.)

# 🧠 Summary (Simple Mental Model)

Think of Granular Certificates like **hourly RECs**.

1.  **Generators** create renewable energy → Emit meter data.
2.  **EACs** (monthly/annual) represent that energy originally.
3.  **GC Issuers** break EACs into **hourly certificates** using verified meter data and audit standards.
4.  **Registries** store these GCs and expose them via API.
5.  **Buyers** (your users) acquire GCs (either from their own PPAs or from marketplaces).
6.  **Your app** imports these certificates and matches them to the user’s hourly consumption gaps.

This is all **digital**, **API-driven**, and **requires no hardware** on your side.
